## The Task App Landscape

There are hundreds of task apps. Most people have tried at least five. And yet most people still feel like their task system isn't quite working.

The problem isn't that existing apps are bad. It's that they're solving a different problem than the one most people actually have.

**Todoist, Things, and TickTick are excellent list managers.** They help you capture, organize, and retrieve tasks. If your problem is "I forget things" or "My tasks are scattered," any of these will help.

But if your problem is "I know what I need to do, but I don't know what to do *first*" or "I start my day overwhelmed," a list manager won't solve it. You need something that thinks with you, not just stores for you.

## The Comparison

### Todoist

**Strengths:** Cross-platform, natural language input, excellent integrations, strong free tier.

**Best for:** People who need to capture tasks quickly across many contexts and don't mind a utilitarian interface.

**Where First Light differs:** Todoist is passive. It waits for you to add, organize, and prioritize. First Light actively generates a morning briefing from your data. Todoist has AI features now, but they're bolted on — ask the AI to help, and it will. First Light's AI is the core experience.

**Verdict:** Use Todoist if you want a powerful list manager. Use First Light if you want a thinking partner.

### Things

**Strengths:** Beautiful design, thoughtful UX, excellent Apple ecosystem integration, "Today" view that actually works.

**Best for:** Apple users who value design and want a calm, focused task experience.

**Where First Light differs:** Things is the best-designed traditional task app. But it's still traditional — tasks go in, tasks come out, the app doesn't do anything with them. First Light has a similar design philosophy (calm, focused, no clutter) but adds AI that actively helps you decide what to work on.

**Verdict:** Use Things if you want the best traditional task app on Apple. Use First Light if you want that same calm design with an AI brain.

### TickTick

**Strengths:** Feature-rich, includes calendar and habits, good cross-platform support, affordable premium.

**Best for:** People who want one app for tasks, calendar, and habits without paying much.

**Where First Light differs:** TickTick does more things than First Light — it has a calendar, a habit tracker, a pomodoro timer, and more. But it does all of them adequately, not exceptionally. First Light focuses on doing one thing exceptionally well: helping you plan and execute your day with AI assistance.

**Verdict:** Use TickTick if you want an all-in-one toolkit. Use First Light if you want a specialized daily planning experience.

## What First Light Does Differently

**1. The Daily Edition**

No other app generates a personalized morning briefing from your actual data. This isn't a generic "good morning" message — it's a structured analysis of your day, priorities, and patterns.

**2. MCP Integration**

First Light is built on MCP (Model Context Protocol), which means AI assistants can read and write your data. Your task app becomes part of your AI workflow, not a separate tool.

**3. Goal-Aware Planning**

Tasks in First Light can be linked to goals. The AI understands these connections and factors them into recommendations. "Finish the Q3 review" isn't just a task — it's a step toward "Get promoted to Senior PM."

**4. Semantic Search**

Find tasks by meaning, not just keyword. Search "things I keep putting off" and get actual results. This requires vector embeddings and AI — not something you can bolt onto a traditional task app.

## What First Light Doesn't Do (Yet)

We're honest about our limitations:

- **No native mobile apps yet.** The web app is responsive and works as a PWA, but native iOS/Android apps are on the roadmap.
- **Fewer integrations than Todoist.** We connect to Google Calendar and offer MCP, but we don't have the hundreds of Zapier integrations that Todoist has.
- **No kanban/board view.** We focus on list and calendar views. If you need Trello-style boards, we're not there yet.
- **Smaller team.** We're a small team building carefully, not a large company shipping features weekly.

## Who Should Use What

**Use Todoist if:** You need the most powerful, flexible task manager with the most integrations. You're willing to do your own prioritization.

**Use Things if:** You're deep in the Apple ecosystem, you value beautiful design, and you want a calm, focused task experience.

**Use TickTick if:** You want one cheap app that does tasks, calendar, and habits adequately.

**Use First Light if:** You want AI to actively help you plan your day, you value integration with AI assistants, and you're willing to trade some flexibility for a more opinionated, guided experience.
